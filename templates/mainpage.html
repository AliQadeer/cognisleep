Meet Dr. Cogni

Sleep Physician Created | Based On Science | Easy To Use Approach

CogniSleep partners with physicians to help you harness the power of your thoughts through Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT-I): a program backed by clinical studies and years of research.

Six weeks may be all you need

If you have problems falling asleep or staying asleep, CogniSleep can help you. It’s a provider-guided program that can help you develop the skills necessary to combat insomnia in as few as 6 weeks without the use of sleeping pills.

We understand your sleeplessness

This program was designed by Dr. Dacelin St. Martin, a triple Board Certified Physician, respected in the fields of Sleep Medicine, Pediatrics, and Internal Medicine. Here are a few words from him about CogniSleep: “CogniSleep is a revolutionary program for insomnia that has proven to be extremely effective. It is based on the principles of Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia, or CBT-I, and has been proven to be more effective than sleeping pills; has results than can last a lifetime; and has no side effects. I strongly encourage all insomnia sufferers to try CogniSleep.”

CogniSleep Disclaimer

It is the intent of Cognisleep to help patients who suffer from insomnia. The program is meant to be used after an initial evaluation by a trained, qualified medical professional. It is not meant to replace or substitute the judgment of a qualified medical professional. Instead, it is supplemental support in the overall management of a patient with insomnia. CogniSleep is a way to re-train your brain, but it cannot cure all sleep disorders, including, but not limited to, sleep apnea, restless leg syndrome, narcolepsy, or depression. These issues must be addressed by a medical or mental health professional. If this program asks you to do anything that has previously been advised against by a medical professional, please contact your healthcare provider before following the program. If during the course of the program you develop any life-threatening conditions, such as suicidal thoughts or other related conditions, please call 988 if you are in the United States.
